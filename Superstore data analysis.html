Introduction

This is an exploratory and descriptive analysis of this Superstore’s customers, products, segments, and regions to better understand what and who to target or avoid in future campaigns, thereby providing data-driven insights.

Based on the dataset, we will be attempting to answer the following, but also will answer more questions as they arise through analysis:

  1. Which products are the best selling? Least selling?

    • Which products are the most profitable? Least profitable?

  2. What product (sub)categories have the highest sales? Lowest sales?

    • Which product (sub)categories are the most profitable? Least profitable?

  3. What is the composition of the customer base?

    • By segment?

    • By region?

Conclusions and Recommendations

Conclusions:

  1. Most sales and profits are to Consumers, and in the Eastern and Western Regions.

  2. Technology tends to have the best total and average sales and profits. Furniture tends to under-perform relative to other product categories.

    1. Copiers and phones are the most profitable, and tables and machines tend to net losses.

  3. Most products remain profitable so long as the discount offered is no more than 30 percent off.

    1. Machines see the most drastic decreases in profit margin when the discount offered increases.

  4. Sales and profits tend to be best across each region and segment in September, November, and December, likely due to back to school and holiday shopping.

  5. Customers prefer standard shipping to other shipping methods.

  6. Using the linear model, we can confidently predict that greater quantity sold will generate more profits for most product subcategories, aside from tables.

Recommendations:

  1. Maintain as is for the Consumer segment, and East and West regions. Give customers in remaining regions and segments incentives to shop with 15-30% discounts to increase spending without sacrificing profits too much.
    1. Keep prices and quantities as is for September, November, and December. Incentivize spending in other months with small discounts.
  2. Copiers are by far the most profitable, and become more profitable as more are sold, so there is minimal need for providing discounts. 1. Consider not selling tables anymore. The margins are bad and tend to lose the Superstore money.
